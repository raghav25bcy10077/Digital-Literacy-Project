File Name: infugraphic.png

Target Audience: Batchmates / Students

Core Theme: Digital Literacy Fundamentals

🎨 Design Overview
The infographic is structured into three distinct sections to ensure high readability and information retention:

Definition of Digital Literacy: A foundational section defining the concept as the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using technology.

Useful Digital Tools: Practical recommendations for students, including:

Digital Calendars: For time management and planning.

Search Engines: Emphasizing the need for "smart" searching.

Creative Apps: Encouraging digital content creation and project work.

Safe Internet Practices: A critical safety checklist featuring:

Strong password security.

Mindful clicking (avoiding phishing/malware).

Protection of personal data.

📝 Project Report
Tool Used: Canva

For this task, I used Canva because of its robust library of educational icons and its intuitive drag-and-drop interface, which allowed me to maintain a professional yet approachable aesthetic. My design focuses on the "Essential Three" of digital citizenship: understanding the definition of digital literacy, identifying tools that boost productivity, and establishing a baseline for online safety.

I chose a color palette of teal and gold to symbolize both technology and "bright ideas." One thing I found particularly interesting during the design process was the challenge of "visual hierarchy." I initially tried to include email etiquette as well, but I realized that overcrowding the page made the safety tips less impactful. I had to make the executive decision to cut down the text to ensure the most vital information—like password security—stood out immediately. This taught me that in digital literacy, clarity is often more important than being exhaustive; if a student can’t digest the information in 30 seconds, the resource hasn't done its job.
