Case Study: Ransomware Attack on "Tools & Solutions" 
Ransomware is a type of malicious software designed to block access to a computer system or encrypt its data until a sum of money (ransom) is paid. In 2025, a small business named Tools & Solutions (T&S) became a prime example of how devastating these attacks can be for mid-sized enterprises.
How the Incident Occurred
The attack followed a classic multi-step trajectory:
1.	Initial Compromise: An employee received a high-pressure "urgent invoice" email. Clicking the attached PDF executed a hidden script that bypassed the company’s outdated antivirus.
2.	Lateral Movement: The malware moved through the unsegmented network, identifying the central server and stealing administrative credentials.
3.	Exfiltration and Encryption: Before locking the files, the attackers exported sensitive client data (Double Extortion). Finally, they deployed the ransomware, appending extensions to all business-critical files.
4.	Ransom Demand: A digital note appeared on all screens demanding $150,000 in Bitcoin for the decryption key.

Target and Consequences
While large corporations make headlines, T&S was targeted because small businesses often have weaker cybersecurity infrastructure and fewer backups.
The consequences were severe. T&S suffered 24 days of total operational downtime, leading to a direct revenue loss of nearly $500,000. Beyond the financial hit, the company faced a massive "trust deficit"; 80% of their partners required a full security audit before resuming contracts. T&S ultimately chose not to pay the ransom, instead undergoing a grueling manual data recovery process from fragmented offline records.

Source: Majumdar, P. (2025). Case Study: How T&S Survived a Ransomware Attack. American Journal of Information Science and Technology.
